This paper addresses the practical deployment challenges of reconstruction-based locomotion mode prediction, including reliance on high-performance hardware and wearer cooperation during initialization. By exploiting complementary sensor modalities through a cascaded LiDAR-Visual-Inertial coupling scheme, we progressively eliminate the preconditions required by each modality for gravity alignment and motion state estimation, enabling initialization under both stationary and walking conditions. We further construct a terrain map through progressive plane representation, entirely forgoing point cloud storage in favor of polygon-based planar primitives, achieving GPU-free terrain reconstruction. The overall locomotion mode prediction performance is on par with or exceeds that of GPU-dependent approaches.
